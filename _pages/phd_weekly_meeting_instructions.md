### Proposed meeting structure

#### Before meetings

The student sends **(i) an agenda** and **(ii) related materials** to the project members (e.g., daily supervisor and/or collaborators) **one working day before** the meeting (e.g., by Friday end of day for the Monday meeting).

#### During meetings

The student leads the discussion based on the shared agenda. The student also documents all **action points** (who / when / what).

#### After meetings

Within the same day, the student sends the **updated meeting notes** to the daily supervisor and, if relevant, collaborators. The daily supervisor replies to confirm the notes. This ensures shared understanding of next steps and avoids miscommunication.

---

### Meeting duration

**Maximum 1 hour.**  
Focus on key issues and avoid unnecessary tangents.

---

### Roles & responsibilities

#### PhD student

- Leads the project
- Conducts research
- Coordinates meetings
- Sets deadlines

#### Daily supervisor

- Checks progress
- Discusses data/technical issues
- Decides short-term actions
- Evaluates scientific outputs

#### Promoter

- Provides higher-level direction
- Refines hypotheses and research questions

#### Collaborators

- Support and advise on research execution

---

### Items to include in meeting notes

#### Before the meeting, written by the student

- **Original plan**  
  _What did I plan to do since last time?_

- **Actions taken**  
  _What did I actually do?_

- **Findings**  
  _What did I learn or find?_

- **Questions and issues**  
  _What challenges or uncertainties arose?_

- **Next tasks**  
  _What do I plan to do before the next meeting?_

#### During the meeting, written by the student

- **Decisions made**  
  _What did we agree to work on? What is de-prioritised?_

- **Action points**  
  _Who does what, by when?_
